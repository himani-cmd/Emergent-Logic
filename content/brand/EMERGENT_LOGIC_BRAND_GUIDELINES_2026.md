# Emergent Logic Brand Guidelines

- Version: 1.2
- Effective: 2026-07-14
- Status: Authoritative
- Brand owner and sole public-facing person: Himani Kharbanda

## 1. Purpose

This guide is the source of truth for new Emergent Logic websites, documents, proposals, audits, presentations, social assets, directory profiles, and public communications.

It resolves colour and identity drift found across earlier repository assets. New work must follow this guide. Older assets may remain as historical records, but they are not automatically approved for reuse.

Machine-readable tokens: `content/brand/emergent-logic-brand-tokens.json`

## 2. Brand Foundation

### Brand name

Write the company name as **Emergent Logic** in body copy, headings, metadata, and public profiles.

- Preferred: Emergent Logic
- Compact document label: EMERGENT LOGIC
- Avoid: Emergent Logics, EmergentLogic, EL Consulting, or shortened company names in first reference

### Positioning

Emergent Logic helps Canadian businesses implement, clean up, and automate CRM and lead follow-up systems so ownership, next steps, and reporting remain visible.

### Approved descriptors

Official logo tagline:

> AI-Accelerated Solutions. Human-Centered Results.

Primary descriptor:

> AI-accelerated CRM consulting

Service line:

> CRM implementation, cleanup, automation, and lead capture systems

Campaign line:

> Find the leak. Fix the flow.

The official tagline is fixed artwork in the supplied logo files and may also appear as live text with the same wording and punctuation. Use the primary descriptor outside the logo when service clarity matters. The campaign line belongs to lead follow-up and audit campaigns; it is not a replacement for the official tagline.

### Brand promise

Make the operating path clear, fix a defined bottleneck, test the result, and leave documentation behind.

### Brand character

- Precise, not performative
- Calm, not passive
- Technical, but understandable
- Evidence-led, not inflated
- Helpful before salesy
- AI-accelerated, with human accountability

## 4. Logo System

### Official logo family

The approved identity is the supplied hex-network mark with the uppercase `EMERGENT LOGIC` wordmark and the fixed tagline `AI-Accelerated Solutions. Human-Centered Results.` Do not recreate, retype, isolate, or substitute any part of the logo artwork.

There are exactly two approved masters:

- Dark square 1080: `content/brand/assets/emergent_logic_dark_square_1080.png`
- Primary dark 500: `content/brand/assets/emergent_logic_primary_logo_dark_500.png`

Use the dark square master for high-resolution square placements and branded holding fields. Use the primary dark master for profiles, compact square placements, schema, favicons, and faithful layout derivatives. A derivative may resize the complete artwork uniformly or place it unchanged on an approved layout. It may not crop, redraw, recolour, trace, isolate, or retype the artwork.

### Small placements

There is no approved symbol-only logo. Below the minimum logo size, use `Emergent Logic` as accessible live text without recreating the symbol. Favicons and platform icons are the sole exception: they must be faithful whole-artwork size derivatives generated from the approved primary dark master.

### Clear space

Let `x` equal 25% of the complete artwork width.

- Keep at least `x` of empty space around every side of a standalone symbol.
- For any lockup, keep at least `x` around the complete artwork, including the tagline.
- No text, border, photograph edge, chart, or CTA may enter the clear-space area.

### Minimum size

| Asset | Digital | Print |
|---|---:|---:|
| Dark square or primary dark master | 160 px wide | 42 mm wide |

Below the logo minimum, use `Emergent Logic` as live text. Do not shrink the full logo into interface chrome where the wordmark and tagline become decorative noise.

### Contrast rules

- Light background: place either approved master as its complete navy holding field.
- Dark background: preserve the complete navy holding field and give it a visible boundary or sufficient separation.
- Do not remove the background from either approved master.
- On photography: use a solid light or navy holding field. Do not place the mark directly on a visually busy area.
- Minimum contrast is 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text and meaningful graphics.

## 5. Colour System

The logo component and business-card system establish navy `#1E3A5F` and indigo `#6366F1` as the canonical brand pair. Nearby violet values used in older website and social assets are transitional and must not create additional colour variants.

### Core colours

| Token | HEX | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Brand Navy | `#1E3A5F` | Brand anchor, dark surfaces, headings |
| Signal Indigo | `#6366F1` | Logo nodes, diagrams, large accents |
| Action Indigo | `#4B37C8` | Accessible buttons, links, active states |
| Deep Ink | `#101828` | Primary text |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Reverse text and clean surfaces |

Signal Indigo is part of the mark, but white text on it narrowly misses the 4.5:1 normal-text target. Use Action Indigo behind normal-size white button text.

### Supporting colours

| Token | HEX | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Muted Slate | `#667085` | Secondary text |
| Soft Indigo | `#EEF2FF` | Tinted panels and diagrams |
| Paper | `#FBFBFF` | Primary light background |
| Line | `#DFE3F5` | Borders and dividers |
| Process Teal | `#0F766E` | Verified, complete, and process signals |
| Alert Coral | `#D95D39` | Risk, bottleneck, and attention signals |

### Colour proportions

For a typical light asset:

- 65-80% Paper or White
- 10-20% Brand Navy or Deep Ink
- 5-10% Indigo family
- Up to 5% combined Teal and Coral

For a dark asset, Brand Navy may become the dominant field. Keep Indigo, Teal, and Coral as controlled signals rather than full-page gradients.

### Colour restrictions

- Do not introduce new violet, blue, or purple shades when an approved token works.
- Do not make Signal Indigo, Teal, or Coral the body-text colour.
- Alert Coral supports large labels, rules, icons, and pale-tint panels; do not use normal-size white text on solid Coral.
- Do not use gradients inside a logo.
- Do not build one-note assets dominated by near-identical purple shades.
- No Pantone colour is assigned. For commercial printing, request a printer proof from the supplied RGB/HEX artwork before production.

## 6. Typography

### Primary typeface

Use **Inter** for websites, social graphics, presentations, and editable digital documents.

Fallback stack:

`Arial, Helvetica, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, Segoe UI, sans-serif`

For portable PDFs, Arial or Helvetica is approved when Inter cannot be embedded.

### Weights

- 800: display statements and major report covers
- 700: headings, wordmark, buttons, key numbers
- 600: subheadings, labels, table headers
- 500: navigation and emphasis
- 400: body copy

Do not use thin weights. Letter spacing is always `0`; do not use negative tracking. The wordmark and tagline inside approved logo files are fixed artwork, not editable typography styles.

### Web scale

| Style | Size / line height |
|---|---|
| Display | 48 / 56 px |
| H1 | 40 / 48 px |
| H2 | 30 / 38 px |
| H3 | 22 / 30 px |
| Body | 16 / 26 px |
| Small | 14 / 22 px |
| Label | 12 / 16 px |

On mobile, use 36/44 for display and 32/40 for H1. Do not scale type continuously with viewport width.

### Document scale

| Style | Size / line height |
|---|---|
| Cover | 32 / 38 pt |
| Section | 18 / 23 pt |
| Subsection | 13 / 17 pt |
| Body | 10 / 15 pt |
| Caption | 8 / 11 pt |

### Writing and case

- Use sentence case for headings, buttons, and navigation.
- Use title case only for the company name, formal document titles, and proper names.
- Use all caps only for short labels of three words or fewer.
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- Use numerals for operational metrics, time, prices, and process steps.

## 7. Interface And Website Layout

Emergent Logic interfaces should feel quiet, operational, and easy to scan.

### Structure

- Use full-width page bands with a constrained content column.
- Put the actual service, CRM state, report finding, workflow, or offer in the first viewport.
- Keep a visible hint of the next section on landing-page viewports.
- Use cards only for repeated items, comparisons, or framed tools.
- Never place cards inside cards.
- Keep card and button radii at 8 px or less.
- Use icons for familiar tools and actions; use text buttons for clear commands.

### Buttons

- Primary: Action Indigo background, white text.
- Secondary: White or transparent background, Brand Navy text, Line or white border depending on surface.
- Tertiary: Action Indigo text link with a visible hover/focus treatment.
- Use one primary CTA per section.

### Imagery

- Prefer real CRM screens, workflow diagrams, report excerpts, and actual deliverables.
- Images must help the viewer inspect the product, process, or result.
- Avoid generic office stock, glowing AI brains, robots, decorative gradient blobs, and fabricated dashboards.
- Client logos, screenshots, and metrics require permission or must be clearly marked as samples.

## 8. Document And PDF Layout

### Page setup

- Use US Letter portrait for audits and one-page reviews.
- Use US Letter landscape for presentations and brand/reference guides.
- Maintain at least 0.5 inch margins.
- Use a navy first-page header or cover with a high-contrast approved lockup.
- Place the document title, subject, prepared date, and scope near the top.

### Content structure

1. Context or opportunity
2. Evidence or public observation
3. Operational implication
4. Recommended next step
5. Scope and methodology note

### Visual rules

- One key message per panel.
- No nested cards.
- Use 6-8 px corner radii.
- Use Coral for risks and Teal for verified or completed states.
- Use one CTA block per page at most.
- Use live, clickable links in exported PDFs.
- Footer every page with Emergent Logic, website, version/date, and page number where appropriate.

### Public identity in documents

Use `Prepared by Himani Kharbanda, Founder & CEO` when a personal byline is needed. Use the organization-only footer for research reports where the company is the author.

## 9. Social Layouts

### Approved formats

- LinkedIn portrait: 1080 x 1350 px, 80 px safe margin
- LinkedIn or Instagram square: 1080 x 1080 px, 72 px safe margin
- Open Graph: 1200 x 630 px, 64 px safe margin

### Composition

- Top zone: one complete approved master at legible size, or the company name as live text without a reconstructed symbol
- Primary zone: one clear claim, question, number, or operator insight
- Evidence zone: one diagram, checklist, source note, or real deliverable excerpt
- Footer zone: one CTA and the domain

### Social copy on image

- Aim for one headline and one supporting line.
- Keep the core image copy under 30 words when possible.
- Use 42-64 px for portrait headlines and 28-36 px for supporting copy.
- Never use body-size text to compensate for too much content.
- Use Himani for founder posts and the company page for organizational posts.
- Do not put another person's name, photo, quote, or profile on brand-owned social assets without explicit approval.

### Social visual style

- Default to Paper or White backgrounds with Brand Navy text and controlled Indigo accents.
- Use data, process diagrams, and deliverable excerpts as the visual proof.
- Avoid decorative one-note purple fields, fake chat screenshots, generic lifestyle imagery, and crowded icon grids.

## 10. Tone Of Voice

### Voice principles

1. **Specific before impressive.** Name the field, stage, workflow, owner, or next step.
2. **Systems before tools.** Explain the operating path before recommending software.
3. **Evidence before claims.** Show the source, method, or observable condition.
4. **Calm before urgent.** State consequences without manufacturing fear.
5. **Helpful before salesy.** Give the reader a useful next step before asking for a call.
6. **Human accountability.** Describe AI as acceleration, not a substitute for ownership or judgment.

### Preferred language

- map the current state
- make ownership visible
- fix one defined bottleneck
- test the handoff
- document the operating process
- directional sample
- public observation
- fit check
- clear success criteria

### Avoid

- revolutionary
- game-changing
- guaranteed revenue
- effortless automation
- fully autonomous growth
- dominate your market
- replace your team
- secret loophole
- vague superlatives without evidence

### Example transformation

Avoid:

> Revolutionize your revenue with an autonomous AI CRM system.

Use:

> We map the handoff, fix the routing or workflow, test it, and document the result.

### Calls to action

Preferred:

- Book a 15-minute fit check
- Get a free 5-point audit
- Show me the CRM issue
- Review the source table
- See whether the sprint fits

Avoid pressure language, false scarcity, or guaranteed outcomes.

## 11. Incorrect Usage

Do not:

- Stretch, skew, crop, rotate, outline, bevel, glow, or shadow a logo.
- Recolour logo nodes or lines outside approved variants.
- Reconstruct, retype, rearrange, or replace the supplied wordmark or tagline.
- Use an `EL` letter roundel as an Emergent Logic logo.
- Use a reconstructed or symbol-only hex mark.
- Separate the wordmark, tagline, or symbol from an approved lockup to invent a new composition.
- Use a low-resolution screenshot when a source component or high-resolution export exists.
- Treat a compatibility alias under `public/el-logo-*` or `public/hex-logo-original.png` as a new master.
- Reuse the legacy `public/og-image.png` copy without updating it to the approved descriptor and palette.
- Create additional violet shades from legacy website or social assets.
- Use another personal identity in founder posts, signatures, bios, outreach, business cards, or social assets.
- Claim a company is a client when it is only a public research source or outreach prospect.
- Publish invented metrics, testimonials, logos, or before-and-after results.
- Put white normal-size text on Signal Indigo or Alert Coral without checking contrast.
- Use all-caps paragraphs, negative letter spacing, oversized card headings, or text that overflows its container.
