# Growth Operations QA Checklist

Prepared: 2026-06-29  
Owner: QA / Reviewer Agent  
Scope: BDR, social, SEO/AEO/GEO, backlink, and growth-ops outputs

## Purpose

This checklist is the final gate before any Emergent Logic growth asset is trusted, imported, drafted, posted, published, or submitted.

The goal is simple: protect the brand, avoid bad outreach, keep claims honest, and make sure every growth action supports revenue instead of creating noise.

## Top Risks Found

### 1. Brand Drift

Risk: Some social graphics and templates can drift into generic SaaS visuals: loud gradients, neon colors, oversized abstract shapes, or agency-style hype.

Required correction:

- Use navy, white, muted blue, soft gray, and clean spacing.
- Use the EL circle mark as the main brand mark.
- Keep graphics restrained, practical, and premium.
- Do not use loud gradients, emoji-heavy visuals, or generic "AI agency" styling.

### 2. Wrong Sender

Risk: Outbound drafts have previously landed in Himani's Gmail instead of Bhavuk's Gmail.

Required correction:

- BDR outreach drafts must be created from `bhavuk.sood@emergent-logic.ca`.
- Do not draft from Himani's inbox unless the task explicitly says Himani should send.
- If there is doubt, run one test draft first and visually confirm the sender mailbox.

### 3. Blocked Contacts

Risk: Prospecting and automation can accidentally include blocked contacts or the user's current employer.

Required correction:

- Never contact BigBang360.
- Never contact any `@bigbang360.com` email.
- Never contact Doug Cheetham, MC Renovation and Painting, or `mc@paintandreno.com`.
- If a blocked contact appears anywhere, stop that row and flag it.

### 4. Generic Outreach

Risk: Quantity-driven BDR work creates weak emails to generic inboxes with no real pain signal.

Required correction:

- Named contacts first.
- Generic inboxes only when the public pain signal is strong.
- Every draft needs one specific observation about the company.
- Do not claim HubSpot, Salesforce, or CRM usage unless a public source confirms it.
- Keep cold emails short, specific, and easy to reply to.

### 5. Claims Too Strong

Risk: Case studies, SEO pages, and social posts can sound like proven client outcomes when they are actually representative/internal implementation patterns.

Required correction:

- Use "representative workflow", "example implementation", "internal build pattern", or "common operating pattern" when not describing a real client.
- Do not claim revenue lift, time savings, accuracy, or client results unless documented.
- Avoid phrases like "guaranteed", "best", "10x", "rank quickly", or "zero risk" unless directly supported.

### 6. Too Much Content Too Fast

Risk: Posting too often can make the company look automated, desperate, or inconsistent.

Required correction:

- LinkedIn profile: maximum 1 thoughtful founder post per business day.
- LinkedIn company page: maximum 1 company post per business day.
- Instagram: reuse the same idea only when converted into a visual format, not copied blindly.
- Do not publish more than one heavy carousel per day.

### 7. Weak SEO / Backlink Quality

Risk: Directory and backlink work can become low-quality if it targets spammy directories or creates thin duplicated profiles.

Required correction:

- Prioritize HubSpot marketplace, Google Business Profile, local Surrey/Vancouver citations, credible B2B directories, and relevant industry profiles.
- Avoid gambling, adult, crypto-spam, coupon, scraper, or irrelevant directories.
- Keep descriptions consistent but not copy-pasted word-for-word.
- Track submitted, pending, approved, rejected, and live links.

### 8. Missing Approval Gates

Risk: Automation may import, draft, post, or submit without enough review.

Required correction:

- Prospect rows default to `approved_for_outreach = No`.
- Drafting requires a specific pain signal and a valid sender.
- Sending remains separate from drafting unless intentionally enabled.
- Social posts and graphics must pass brand review before posting.
- Backlink submissions must pass relevance review before submission.

## Universal Pass / Fail Checklist

Every output must pass these checks before it moves forward.

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Brand fit | Looks and sounds like Emergent Logic: calm, practical, premium, CRM-first |
| Sender | Uses `bhavuk.sood@emergent-logic.ca` for BDR unless explicitly assigned otherwise |
| Blocklist | No BigBang360, no `@bigbang360.com`, no Doug Cheetham, no MC Renovation |
| Specificity | Includes a real company, workflow, service, or pain signal |
| Claim safety | No unsupported metrics, guarantees, or fake client-result claims |
| CTA | Low-friction and practical: review, audit, contact, or strategy call |
| Link quality | Uses clean site links, not long raw URLs unless necessary |
| Approval gate | Draft/import/post/submit state is clear before action |

If any item fails, the output does not ship.

## BDR Output Checklist

Before importing a prospect:

- Company is not blocked.
- Email domain is not `bigbang360.com`.
- Company fits at least one target motion: CRM cleanup, HubSpot, Salesforce, RevOps, lead routing, automation, intake, or follow-up.
- There is a public pain signal or complexity signal.
- Segment, offer angle, and notes are filled.
- `approved_for_outreach` stays `No` until reviewed.

Before creating a Gmail draft:

- Sender is `bhavuk.sood@emergent-logic.ca`.
- Contact is preferably a named person.
- If using a generic inbox, the company has a strong reason to contact.
- Email is 90-130 words where possible.
- First line references one concrete observation.
- No long raw URLs unless directly useful.
- No "I noticed you use HubSpot/Salesforce" unless sourced.
- CTA asks for a small review, not a hard sales call.

Before sending:

- Draft is visually checked in Bhavuk's Gmail.
- Recipient is not blocked.
- Subject is not spammy.
- Tone is respectful and specific.
- HubSpot logging/tracking is acceptable for that send.

## Social Output Checklist

Before creating a social graphic:

- Uses navy, white, muted blue, soft gray.
- Uses EL circle mark or a subtle approved brand element.
- One main idea only.
- Enough whitespace.
- No loud generic SaaS gradients.
- No excessive icons, emojis, or stock-business style.
- The graphic can work as LinkedIn square, Instagram square, or portrait carousel with minimal adjustment.

Before posting:

- Caption is practical and not hype-driven.
- No unsupported client claims.
- No overposting: check the daily cadence.
- Founder/profile posts sound human and operator-led.
- Company posts are shorter and more service-led.
- Instagram copy is adapted to the visual format, not pasted from LinkedIn unchanged.

## SEO / AEO / GEO Checklist

Before publishing a page or post:

- Page supports a real service or buying-intent keyword.
- Content answers specific operational pain, not generic marketing language.
- H1 and meta title are clear.
- Internal links include at least one relevant service page and one related resource where natural.
- Contact/strategy-call path is visible.
- Claims are soft and supportable.
- If the page is a case study without a real named client, label it as a representative workflow or example implementation.
- No keyword stuffing.
- No fake location claims.

Answer-engine readiness:

- Include direct answers to common questions.
- Use concise headings.
- Add definitions where helpful.
- Explain when a business should and should not use the service.
- Keep language clear enough to be quoted or summarized by AI search tools.

## Backlink / Citation Checklist

Before submitting a profile or link:

- Site is relevant to CRM, technology services, Canadian business, Surrey/Vancouver, HubSpot, Salesforce, SaaS, or small business operations.
- Directory does not look spammy.
- Description is accurate and not over-optimized.
- Link points to the best page: home, service page, contact, or case-study hub.
- Submission is tracked with status and date.
- No fake reviews.
- No paid link schemes.
- No mass-submission directories.

## Automation Checklist

Before running an n8n workflow:

- Workflow name and ID are known.
- Test mode or row cap is used first.
- Sender credential is verified if Gmail is involved.
- Paid tools such as Apify are tested with a small run first.
- Send nodes stay disabled unless intentionally approved.
- Blocklist filter is active before data reaches Google Sheets, HubSpot, Gmail, or posting nodes.
- Output destination is confirmed: Google Sheet, HubSpot, Gmail drafts, LinkedIn, Instagram, or GitHub.

## QA Decision Labels

Use these labels when reviewing agent output:

- `Approved`: ready to import, draft, post, publish, or submit.
- `Needs Edit`: good direction, but requires copy/brand/claim cleanup.
- `Needs Research`: not enough proof or pain signal.
- `Hold`: strategically fine, but not for today.
- `Reject`: off-brand, blocked, risky, spammy, or too generic.

## Minimum Quality Bar By Agent

### BDR Agent

Must produce fewer, better prospects with named contacts and a real reason to reach out.

### SEO / AEO / GEO Content Agent

Must publish content that supports service demand, internal linking, and answer-engine visibility without fake claims.

### Social Creative Agent

Must stay brand-faithful and convert one real pain point into a clean, channel-ready asset.

### Backlink / Citation Agent

Must build reputation through relevant citations, not low-quality links.

### QA / Reviewer Agent

Must stop weak output before it reaches the market.

## Final Rule

If an output looks fast but not credible, stop it.

Emergent Logic should feel like a careful operator-led CRM consultancy, not a content farm or generic AI agency.
