## Himani's 20-Second Introduction

> I'm Himani, founder of Emergent Logic in Surrey. We help small and growing teams find where CRM data, lead routing, or follow-up breaks between tools and people. We usually start with one workflow and give the team a practical repair plan before proposing anything larger. What system does your team use today?

## Discovery Questions

Use one or two naturally, not as a script.

1. Where does a new inquiry go after it first reaches your business?
2. What is the one follow-up or reporting step your team still handles manually?
3. If a lead is missed or delayed, how would you know?
4. Which system is supposed to own that process?
5. Is this something the team wants fixed now, or is it only an inconvenience?

## Qualification Rule

A conversation is qualified only when all three are true:

- The person owns or influences the process.
- They identify a current problem involving CRM data, routing, follow-up, reporting, or automation.
- They agree to receive a specific follow-up.

Do not count business cards, social follows, or vague interest as leads.

## Permission-Based Follow-Up

Send within one business day from Himani's real profile or Emergent Logic email:

> Hi [First name], it was good meeting you at [event]. You mentioned that [specific process] currently [specific problem]. I can send a one-page check for that exact workflow, using only what you shared, so your team can see where ownership or follow-up may be breaking. Would that be useful?

If they say yes, create the one-page diagnostic before asking for a meeting. If they decline or do not reply after one follow-up, stop.

## Event-to-Revenue Tracking

Record only:

- Event and date

- Problem stated in their own words
- Permission granted for follow-up
- Follow-up date
- Reply, diagnostic requested, meeting requested, or closed

No contact is added to a campaign without permission. No event spend is authorized by this file.
