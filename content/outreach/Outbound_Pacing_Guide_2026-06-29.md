## Short Answer

We have not published too much content publicly. Most current output is Gmail drafts and internal prospecting logs.

The real risk is not "too much content" on the website. The risk is sending too many cold emails too quickly or sending dense emails that feel automated.

## Recommended Sending Pace

- Send 8 to 12 emails per business day while the domain is still warming.
- Do not send all drafts in one morning.
- Start with named-contact drafts before generic inbox drafts.
- Pause for 24 hours if bounce rate or negative replies increase.
- If one vertical gets replies, double down there before expanding.

## Priority Order

1. Named-contact drafts with direct public emails.
2. Named-contact drafts where only a public company inbox is available.
3. High-intent RevOps/SaaS drafts with explicit Salesforce/HubSpot hiring signals.
4. Generic property management inboxes with strong owner/tenant/maintenance portal pain.
5. Research-only contacts with no public email should not be emailed until a valid contact method is confirmed.

## Email Style Rules

- 90 to 130 words preferred.
- 5 to 7 short paragraphs max.
- One pain signal only.
- One soft CTA only.
- Avoid long raw URLs in first-touch emails.
- Avoid sounding like a full pitch or service brochure.
- Do not mention too many services at once.

## Better CTA Examples

- "Would it be worth reviewing one inquiry path?"
- "Open to comparing notes on the handoff?"
- "Would a quick workflow review be useful?"
- "Worth a quick look at where follow-up may be leaking?"

## Stop Conditions

Stop or slow sending if:

- Bounce rate rises above 3%.
- A recipient asks not to be contacted.
- A domain or company is connected to BigBang360.
- The same company has already received an email recently.
- The email relies on a guessed personal address.

## Current Operating Rule

BDR can prepare drafts without approval, but live sending should stay paced and intentional until reply patterns are clear.
