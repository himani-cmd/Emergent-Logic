# EviSmart Public RevOps Readiness Brief

Prepared by Emergent Logic on 2026-07-14. This note uses only EviSmart's public Revenue Operations Manager job brief and public leadership information. It is not a claim about EviSmart's internal system.

## Public Signal

EviSmart's live Vancouver role asks one owner to build Marketing Ops, Sales Ops, Success Ops, and BizOps; govern HubSpot; reconcile billing and finance; improve forecasting; establish customer-health and renewal visibility; and design integration architecture.

## Inference to Validate

The highest-risk period is the handoff between current operators and the incoming RevOps owner. A short evidence-building sprint can give that person a reliable starting point without redesigning the system prematurely.

## First Ten Working Days

1. Control plane
   - List systems and owners for lead, opportunity, contract, billing, customer health, and revenue.
   - Decide which system is authoritative for each critical field.
2. Revenue path
   - Walk a small sample from inquiry to closed-won and from customer to renewal.
   - Record stage criteria, ownership changes, SLA expectations, and exceptions.
3. Workflow reliability
   - Inventory the highest-impact workflows, integrations, dependencies, and failure alerts.
   - Test one path with acceptance criteria before changing automation.
4. Leadership visibility
   - Reconcile a baseline set of pipeline and recurring-revenue metrics.
   - Document definitions, refresh timing, and the person accountable for each metric.

## Low-Risk Starting Point

A fixed-scope diagnostic should leave EviSmart with:

- A source-of-truth and field-ownership matrix.
- A map of one lead-to-revenue or renewal path.
- A prioritized workflow and data-risk backlog.
- Acceptance checks for the first implementation sprint.
- A concise handoff pack for the permanent RevOps owner.

## Decision Question

Which path carries the most near-term revenue risk today: lead-to-opportunity, quote-to-cash, or renewal?
