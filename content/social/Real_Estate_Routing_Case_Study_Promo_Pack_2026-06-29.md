## LinkedIn Founder Post

Real estate leads are not all the same.

A seller valuation request is not the same as a buyer asking one listing question.

An open house lead is not the same as a past-client referral.

A long-cycle buyer is not the same as someone ready to list next week.

But inside many teams, these inquiries end up in the same messy places:

- shared inbox
- listing portal notifications
- text messages
- spreadsheet
- CRM notes
- memory

That is where follow-up starts leaking.

A better CRM setup separates:

- buyers
- sellers
- valuation requests
- referrals
- open house leads
- past clients
- inactive but warm leads

Then each path gets an owner, source, timeline, status, and next follow-up.

Real estate follow-up is not only about speed.

It is about staying visible long enough for the lead to become ready.

## Upwork Proposal Proof Paragraph

For real estate CRM work, I would start by mapping the lead paths first: buyer inquiries, seller valuation forms, listing portal leads, open house contacts, referrals, and past clients. Then I would structure the CRM around lead type, source, owner, timeline, status, and next follow-up before adding automation. This prevents warm leads from disappearing after the first reply.
