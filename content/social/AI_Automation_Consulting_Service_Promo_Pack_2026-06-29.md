## Positioning

This service should be positioned as practical workflow automation for real business operations. Avoid vague AI transformation language. The buyer pain is manual work, scattered systems, missed follow-up, messy CRM updates, and no approval gates.

## LinkedIn Founder Post

Most businesses do not need a fully autonomous AI employee.

They need one painful workflow fixed.

For example:

- website leads into CRM
- Gmail drafts from approved prospects
- quote requests into a follow-up queue
- HubSpot tasks after a form submission
- Google Sheets review before CRM import
- support messages separated from sales inquiries
- AI summaries added to contact records

That is where AI automation is actually useful.

Not “let AI run the business.”

More like:

- classify the request
- clean the data
- summarize the context
- create the record
- assign the owner
- draft the follow-up
- wait for human approval
- log what happened
