## LinkedIn Post - Company Page

Most AI chatbots do not fail because the AI is weak.

They fail because the business has no follow-up system behind the conversation.

A visitor asks a question.
The chatbot replies.
Maybe it collects an email.

Then what?

If the conversation does not become a clean CRM record with ownership, source tracking, urgency, service interest, and a next follow-up step, it is just another disconnected inbox.

The useful question is not:
"Can we add an AI chatbot?"

The useful question is:
"Can this chatbot qualify leads and move them into our CRM follow-up process?"

That is where the value is:
- cleaner lead capture
- faster response
- better routing
- better sales context
- fewer warm inquiries slipping away

## LinkedIn Post - Himani Profile

AI chatbots are becoming common on business websites.

But adding the chatbot is not the hard part.

The hard part is making sure the conversation turns into something useful for the team.

For example:
- Was a CRM contact created?
- Was the inquiry qualified?
- Was the right owner assigned?
- Was the follow-up task created?
- Was the source captured?
- Can the team report on chatbot-sourced leads?

If the answer is no, the chatbot may improve the visitor experience while still failing the sales process.
