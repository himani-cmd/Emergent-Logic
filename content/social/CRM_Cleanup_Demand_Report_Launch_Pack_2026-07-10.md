## Post 1 - Himani Founder Launch

We reviewed 100 public HubSpot and Salesforce job and project postings to answer a simple question:

What CRM work are businesses actually trying to fund?

The strongest pattern was not a specific AI tool.

- 71 postings included workflow or automation work
- 57 included reporting or forecasting
- 55 named both HubSpot and Salesforce
- 75 combined at least three operational requirement areas

The practical conclusion: CRM problems rarely arrive one at a time. Routing depends on lifecycle definitions. Reporting depends on clean fields. Automation depends on ownership. AI depends on all of them.

## Post 3 - Himani Methodology Follow-Up

Publishing a chart is easy. Publishing the source table is the part that matters.

For our CRM demand report, we:

- reviewed 100 public job and project postings
- removed duplicate source URLs
- required every item to mention HubSpot or Salesforce
- coded each posting across nine operational requirement categories
- kept the categories overlapping because one project can involve routing, reporting, workflow, data, and integration at the same time

This is a directional sample, not a market-size claim. It is useful because readers can inspect the public sources and decide how much weight to give the findings.
