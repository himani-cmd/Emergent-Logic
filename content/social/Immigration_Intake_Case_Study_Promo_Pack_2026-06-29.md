## LinkedIn Founder Post

Immigration firms do not usually lose consultation opportunities because people stop needing help.

They lose them because intake is scattered.

A new inquiry might come through:

- website form
- phone call
- WhatsApp
- email
- referral
- social message
- Google Business Profile

Then active client questions and document requests arrive in the same channels.

That creates noise.

The fix is not "more automation" first.

The fix is a clearer intake path:

new inquiry -> CRM record -> service interest -> source -> owner -> consultation status -> next follow-up.

Automation can help after that.

It can route, remind, and make the next action visible.

But it should not replace professional review or legal judgment.

Good CRM work makes the practice easier to run.

It does not pretend to be the consultant.
