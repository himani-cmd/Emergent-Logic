## LinkedIn Founder Post

Salesforce automation does not fix a messy Salesforce org.

It usually scales the mess.

Before building another Flow, check:

- duplicate leads and contacts
- unclear owner assignment
- inactive users owning records
- old fields still used in reports
- inconsistent opportunity stages
- legacy Workflow Rules or Process Builder logic
- dashboards nobody fully trusts

If those inputs are messy, automation only makes bad handoffs happen faster.

The safer sequence:

Audit the org.
Clean the revenue-facing fields.
Map ownership and routing.
Validate reporting definitions.
Then build the automation.

Salesforce is powerful.

But clean process comes before clever Flow.

## Email Proof Paragraph

## Upwork Proposal Proof Paragraph

For Salesforce automation work, I would start by auditing the existing org before building new Flow logic. That means checking objects, fields, duplicate records, assignment rules, inactive owners, reports, validation rules, legacy automation, and integrations. If the foundation is messy, new automation often makes the process harder to maintain. My approach is cleanup-first, then automation.
